Continuous Time-Varying Emotion Control for Zero-Shot TTS

This page presents demo samples for Emotion Orthogonal LoRA (EO-LoRA), a method for continuous and time-varying emotion control in flow-matching-based zero-shot text-to-speech. The approach injects Valence, Arousal, and Dominance (VAD) signals through three aligned low-rank branches, and further improves controllability with a preference-based alignment stage, Flow-DGPO.

Overview

The paper studies continuous and time-varying emotion control in zero-shot TTS using VAD-conditioned low-rank adaptation.

EO-LoRA

Three separate low-rank branches are aligned with Valence, Arousal, and Dominance. Each branch is scaled by its corresponding control signal at each time step.

Time-varying control

Frame-level VAD sequences allow emotion to evolve within an utterance, enabling transitions such as calm → fearful or angry → calm.

Flow-DGPO

A preference-based reinforcement learning stage improves emotion controllability while preserving intelligibility and speaker similarity.

Ethics statement

This demo is intended for research presentation only. Speech synthesis systems with strong speaker similarity and emotional control may be misused for impersonation or deceptive content generation. Any public release should include appropriate safeguards, consent procedures, and misuse mitigation measures.
